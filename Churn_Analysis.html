🚨 Products 3 & 4 are a trap
Customers with 3 products churn at 82.7%; those with 4 churn at 100%. Over-selling is destroying retention. Investigate product bundling strategy urgently.
⚠️ Germany is an outlier
Germany's churn rate of 32.4% is double France's (16.2%) with similar customer counts. Indicates a regional service, pricing, or competitive issue specific to Germany.
🔴 Age 51–60 is the danger zone
Customers aged 51–60 churn at 56.2% — the highest of any group. Inactive members in this bracket hit 85.7% churn. Retention programs should target this cohort first.
⚡ Inactive members at risk
Inactive members churn at 26.9% vs 14.3% for actives. Re-engagement campaigns could recover a significant portion of the 4,849 inactive customers.
✅ 2-product customers are the anchor
Customers with exactly 2 products churn at just 7.6% — the sweet spot. Cross-selling one additional product to single-product holders could dramatically improve retention.
Female customers churn at 25.1% vs 16.5% for males. The gap is consistent across all geographies, suggesting a systemic product-fit or service quality issue for female customers.
